What happens to stolen art? It is fed to AI 
I trained my own neural network on my laptop on my own stolen drawings. To use the tools that have robbed so many artists to resurrect some simulacra of the artwork that was stolen from me has this same ironical taste as turning the tools of collection and extraction back on the digital platform’s ecostructure.
Animations were generated by creating physics simulations and feedback loops in the model’s latent space. A ‘re-spatialisation’ of ideas: first I attempted to draw spatial representations for system dynamics, then my neural networks attempt to find a 96-number representation of the drawing, which is treated as an abstract space by the animations created. 
I believe that high-dimensionality is often used as a form of intellectual intimidation, by treating the 96-number representation as a space, did I contribute to this? I hoped to re-ground the videos by surrounding them with noisy electronics and the clicking sounds of a tightening rope. But I feel the link may not have been strong enough.
